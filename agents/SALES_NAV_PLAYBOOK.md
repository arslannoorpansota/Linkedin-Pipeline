# Playbook: Sales Navigator Targeting & Channel Strategy

> Goal: close a paying client **fast** (cash in 30–60 days) under a hard limit of
> **50 InMail credits/month**. The scarce resource isn't InMails — it's
> reply-to-close rate. Aim 50 sharp messages at funded, hungry buyers instead of
> spraying. Reserve InMails only for targets with no free path in (see §4).

---

## 1. The ICP (who to aim at)

> **Recently-funded startups (Seed–Series B) that are actively hiring engineers
> and can't fill the roles fast enough.**

Why this one: budget is allocated, the roadmap is urgent, and the person you
message *is* the buyer (no procurement). A dev shop / staff aug solves their pain
faster than a 3-month hire. Faster to revenue than agency retainers or Type B/C
partnerships.

Buying signals, in priority order:
1. Raised a round in the **last 3–12 months**
2. **Hiring engineers** on LinkedIn (budget + unmet need in one signal)
3. **Posted on LinkedIn in last 30 days** (responsive + gives you a hook)
4. **Changed jobs in last 90 days** (new leaders rebuild teams, evaluate vendors)

---

## 2. Sales Navigator filter setup

### 2a. Build the ACCOUNT search FIRST (this is the high-leverage move)

The funding + hiring signals **do not exist in the Lead view** — they are
**Account-level** filters. Find the right companies first, then find people in them.

On the **Accounts** tab, set:
- **Company headcount:** `11–50` (sweet spot) + optionally `51–200`
- **Recent activities → Funding events:** raised in **last 3–12 months**
- **Job opportunities → Hiring on LinkedIn:** yes, **Department = Engineering**
- **Company headcount growth:** high
- **Company HQ location:** see §3
- **Industry:** Software Development, Financial Services, Hospitals & Health Care
  (add SaaS / E-commerce / MarTech as you widen)

→ **Save the result as an Account list** (e.g. "Funded + Hiring — Q3").

### 2b. Then run the LEAD search inside that list

On the **Leads** tab:
- **Workflow → Account lists:** select the list from 2a (every lead is now pre-qualified)
- **Seniority level:** CXO, Owner / Partner, Vice President
- **Current job title:** CTO, VP Engineering, Head of Engineering, Co-Founder, Founder
- **Function:** Engineering, Information Technology — **NOT Sales** (see §5)
- **Recent updates → Posted on LinkedIn:** ON
- Run **Changed jobs** as a separate second batch, not stacked on batch one

## 4. Channel decision tree — which path: free vs InMail vs email

**Never spend an InMail where a free path exists.** Triage every lead in this
order and stop at the first match:

| # | If the lead is… | Path to use | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **1st-degree connection** | LinkedIn **direct message** (free) | 0 credits |
| 2 | Has a **shared/mutual connection** | Ask that person for a **warm intro** | 0 credits — converts 3–5× better than cold |
| 3 | Shows an **"Open Profile" badge** | **InMail** — it's **free** on open profiles | 0 credits |
| 4 | In a **LinkedIn Group** you share | **Group message** (free) | 0 credits |
| 5 | **2nd/3rd-degree, no warm path, high fit** | **Connection request** w/ <200-char note FIRST | 0 credits — try this before burning a credit |
| 6 | Connection request ignored 5–7 days, still high fit | **InMail** | 1 credit |
| 7 | You have/can find a **work email** + high fit | **Cold email** from `arslan@electrocomit.com` | 0 credits, unlimited |

Rules of thumb:
- A 50-person list should consume only **~20–25 actual credits** — the rest go via
  free paths above. That effectively doubles your monthly reach.
- **Email is uncapped** — for any lead where you can get a verified work email, prefer
  email over InMail and save the credit. Partner/alliance convos → `partnerships@`.
- Connection note < 200 chars (free-account hard limit). Message < 150 words.

---

## 5. Filter bugs to avoid (these silently break the search)

- **Function must NOT be "Sales."** Setting Function = Sales while job titles are
  CTO/VP Eng contradicts itself and dilutes every result. Use Engineering + IT,
  or clear Function entirely (job titles already target it).
- **Don't set both Company HQ location AND Personal Geography.** They fight:
  e.g. Personal Geography = "North America" silently excludes your Australia
  targets and leaks in offshore-based people (e.g. Pakistan-based founders).
  Keep **Company HQ location only**; clear Personal Geography.

---

## 6. Watch-outs when scanning results

- **Skip competitors.** Staffing / nearshore / offshore / EOR firms are not buyers
  (e.g. a result whose tagline is "We replace nearshore, offshore, and EOR").
  Industry = Software Development surfaces lots of agencies — eyeball before spending.
- **Save credits on warm 2nd-degrees.** A 2nd-degree with mutual connections
  (especially on home turf like Austin, TX) → ask for the intro, don't InMail.

---

## 7. Execution & pacing (the 50 credits)

- **Don't blast.** ~12–15 sends/week, tightly personalized, **one ask each**
  ("Worth a 20-min call?").
- **Measure after the first 15.** If reply rate is **< ~15%**, fix targeting or
  opener *before* spending the rest. The 50 cap forces you to learn from batch one.
- **Reserve ~5 credits** as dry powder for high-value leads that surface mid-month.
- Lead with what you noticed about **them**, never a pitch. Write as Arslan (CTO),
  "I" not "we." (See CLAUDE.md §9 Tone Rules.)

---

## 8. 10-minute quick-start checklist

1. [ ] Accounts tab → funding (3–12 mo) + hiring engineers + headcount 11–50 → **save as Account list**
2. [ ] Leads tab → set Account list = that list
3. [ ] Function → Engineering + IT (remove **Sales**)
4. [ ] Clear Personal Geography; set Company HQ to the §3 Tier-1/2 list (no UK)
5. [ ] Turn ON "Posted on LinkedIn"
6. [ ] Triage each lead through §4 table — free path first, InMail last
7. [ ] Send 12–15/week, log every touch in the BD Pipeline sheet, review reply rate at 15
