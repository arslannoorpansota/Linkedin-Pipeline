## Post 2 — AI/dev point of view

> A hard lesson from building with LLMs: the model is rarely what makes an AI project fail.
>
> What actually breaks things:
> • Retrieval that drifts out of date, so answers quietly go wrong
> • Token costs that balloon the moment real users show up
> • No eval loop, so nobody notices quality dropped until a customer does
>
> The model is maybe 20% of the work. The 80% is the pipeline, the cost control, and the infrastructure around it. That's the part we obsess over — because that's the part that decides whether your AI feature is a demo or a product.
>
